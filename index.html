The Essence Behind the Collection

The Spring 2024 collection of dresses captures the vibrant essence of nature through a stunning interplay of mosaics, colorful patterns, and floral motifs. Each piece is a celebration of renewal, where bold hues and intricate designs converge to evoke the beauty of blossoming gardens. The collection draws inspiration from the rich textures and shapes found in mosaic art, transforming them into fluid silhouettes that dance with movement. As the dresses flutter in the breeze, they invite wearers to embrace the joy of spring, reflecting a harmonious blend of artistry and the effervescent spirit of the season.

Our Designers

Floriana Mosaic is renowned for her ability to blend art and fashion, drawing inspiration from her travels to vibrant marketplaces around the world. With a background in fine arts, she meticulously studies color theory and texture, infusing each dress with a unique narrative. Vividra Atelier, combines eco-friendly materials with bold, eye-catching designs, making each collection not only stylish but also environmentally conscious. For the Spring 2024 line, Vividra experimented with digital printing techniques, allowing for vibrant floral patterns that celebrate the season's essence while minimizing waste. Together, these designers have created a collection that harmonizes artistry and sustainability, inviting wearers to step into a world of color and creativity.

More About Our Founder

Conna Walker started House of CB at 17 by borrowing $3,880 from her father. Eight years later, the brand, characterized by figure-hugging women's clothes, has over 40 retail locations, 2.5 million followers on social and pulled in $15 million in 2018 profits. Her goal? To hire, support and connect with women.

Our Sustainability Commitment

Over the past few years, we have made the active choice to increase our use of natural based fabrics. This allowed our latest collections to be made from 90% natural fibres such as, Cotton, Lyocell, and Acetate. As we are committed to reducing waste, we decided to only produce our styles in strictly limited quantities. This allows us to avoid overproduction and have a real impact on waste.
